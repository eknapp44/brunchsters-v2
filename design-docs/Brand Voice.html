How Brunchsters sounds

We're the friend you have FOMO for. Unironically pumped that you're hanging out — not pushy, not corporate, not trying too hard. Confident, fun, slightly chaotic, and always rooting for the get-together.

One sentence

If we forget everything else, remember this.

We sound like the friend who's already in the group chat, hyping the brunch — wholesomely excited, never overbearing, lightly funny when it earns the moment.

Who we are. Who we're not.

Four traits, each with the thing we're not letting it become.

Wholesomely hyped

Unironically excited that you're seeing your friends. Like the friend who texts "WAIT YES" instantly to any plan.

Not: cynical, dry, "well, brunch is a construct."

The fun-FOMO friend

You see our copy and want in. We make the gathering feel like the place to be — without ever begging.

Not: needy, pushy, "you HAVE to come."

Lightly funny

Earned humor — never punchlines for their own sake. Like the friend who's quietly the wittiest at the table.

Not: joke-density app, "let's gooooo 💯💯💯."

Tool-aware

We help you do a thing. The thing is brunch. We're useful first, charming second. Notion-style competence.

Not: "let our AI-powered platform leverage your social graph."

How it sounds in real copy

Side-by-side: the way we'd write it vs. ways we wouldn't.

A confirmed brunch — title

✓ Yes

Brunch on. 💕

Confident. Two words. Lets the action speak.

✗ Not us

Your event has been successfully scheduled.

Robot-receipt energy. "Event" is a corporate word.

Inviting friends — CTA

✓ Yes

Round up the crew →

Verb forward, specific. "Crew" is warm without being twee.

✗ Not us

Invite Attendees

Title-case + "attendees" turns it into a calendar.

Nobody RSVP'd yet — host empty state

✓ Yes

It's gone quiet. Send a nudge?

Observation + question. No blame. Easy out.

✗ Not us

No responses yet. Engage your community!

"Engage" and "community" are LinkedIn-speak.

A cancellation — invitee notification

✓ Yes

Sam called it off — said next month for sure.

Carries the host's reason. Sounds like a friend telling you.

✗ Not us

EVENT CANCELLED. No further action required.

Cold + capitalized. Reads like a service outage.

Bringing back a past brunch — banner copy

✓ Yes

Bringing back Easter brunch with Mom — round two 🥂

Sequel framing, warm, has the original title carried through.

✗ Not us

Replanning event "Easter brunch with Mom". Please select a new date.

"Event" + imperative + quoted-string title = receipt voice.

Voting closing soon — push notif

✓ Yes

⏳ Voting closes in 2h. Tartine's leading.

Specific. The leader gives them a reason to tap.

✗ Not us

⚡ Action required: Cast your vote!

"Action required" is enterprise-speak. The ⚡ is fake-urgent.

Tone shifts by moment

Same voice, slightly different volume.

Success

"Brunch on. 💕 Maya & Devon are in."

Hype, warm. Reward the moment.

Gentle nudge

"Sam still needs your vote — Tartine's leading."

Friend-tap, specific, easy ignore.

Empty state

"The table's set. Now invite some people."

Inviting, not lonely. Always has a verb out.

Error

"Couldn't send that just now. We'll try again — your tap stuck."

Plain talk. Take the blame. Reassure.

Cancellation

"Sam called it off. No worries — these things happen."

Gentle, low-stakes, warm.

Confirmation moment

"You're in 🥂 — see you Sunday."

Short. Confident. Tiny celebration.

Replan a past brunch

"Bringing back Easter brunch with Mom — round two 🥂 Just pick a new time."

Carries the memory. Frames it as a sequel, not a copy.

Tagline

Your pick (#1) flagged. The rest are alternates by mood.

★ "Your friends are hungry. Probably." Your pick. Confident, low-stakes, makes you smile.
02 "Brunch, but the planning part is fun now." Functional pitch with a wink.
03 "Get the gang together. We'll handle the rest." Service-y, warm. Good for the App Store.
04 "Sunday's already booked, somewhere." Slightly mysterious, very brunch-coded.
05 "For when 'we should brunch' actually happens." For audiences who've sent that text 100 times.
